━━━ GEO WRITING STANDARDS ━━━

WIKI-VOICE — All factual sentences must be written in neutral, third-person, declarative style:
- No "we/our/us/I" in factual statements.
- No subjective phrases ("the best", "amazing", "incredible", "world-class").
- Write declaratively: "The standard procedure is X" — not "We recommend X."
- BAD: "Our experts will handle all your needs efficiently."
- GOOD: "Professional concrete sealing reduces moisture absorption by up to 95%, extending surface life by 5–10 years."

ISLAND TEST — Apply to every sentence:
- Every sentence must make complete sense if extracted with no surrounding context.
- Replace all pronouns (it, they, this, these) with the actual noun.
- BAD: "This is important because it causes long-term damage."
- GOOD: "Untreated mold growth on concrete driveways causes permanent surface staining within 18–24 months."

FACT DENSITY — Ground content in specific data:
- Use measurements, percentages, temperatures, timeframes, and quantities wherever accurate.
- Each bullet in "Key Facts" must contain a number, measurement, or named technique.

QUESTION-FORMAT HEADINGS:
